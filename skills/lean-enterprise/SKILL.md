# Lean Enterprise

## Scope

This reference covers lean thinking applied at the enterprise level -- multi-team, multi-product, portfolio-scale organizations. Where lean management addresses single-site or single-value-stream leadership, lean enterprise addresses the coordination, governance, and strategic challenges of applying lean across complex organizations.

## Core Concepts

### The Three Horizons

Enterprises must manage three investment horizons simultaneously:

- **Horizon 1:** Exploit -- Optimize and extend the core business. Incremental improvement. This is where traditional lean (TPS, lean management) excels.
- **Horizon 2:** Extend -- Scale emerging opportunities that show promise. Growth and expansion of validated ideas.
- **Horizon 3:** Explore -- Experiment with new, unproven ideas. This is lean startup territory -- high uncertainty, small bets, rapid learning.

Lean enterprise manages the portfolio across all three horizons, applying the appropriate methods to each (not applying Horizon 1 optimization methods to Horizon 3 exploration problems, and vice versa).

### Mission Command

An approach to organizational alignment borrowed from military doctrine: leaders set the intent (what to achieve and why) but leave the how to the teams closest to the work. This contrasts with detailed command (specifying exactly how to accomplish objectives).

Mission command requires: clear intent communicated to all levels, competent teams capable of independent action, trust between levels, rapid feedback on results. It is hoshin kanri's catchball principle extended to operational execution.

### Value Stream Networks

At enterprise scale, value streams interact -- they share resources, serve overlapping customers, and depend on common platforms. Managing these interactions requires:
- **Value stream mapping at portfolio level** -- Understanding how value flows across the enterprise, where streams intersect, and where shared dependencies create bottlenecks.
- **Decoupled value streams** -- Organizing teams and architecture so that value streams can deliver independently. Shared dependencies (platforms, services, teams) are the enterprise equivalent of shared resources on a factory floor -- they create queuing, waiting, and coordination waste.
- **Internal platform teams** as services -- Platform teams serve product teams as internal suppliers, with pull-based work intake and service-level expectations.

### Continuous Compliance and Governance

Traditional governance (stage gates, review boards, annual planning cycles) creates batch-and-queue at the organizational level. Lean enterprise replaces these with:
- **Continuous compliance** -- Automate compliance checks into the delivery pipeline rather than batching compliance reviews at milestones.
- **Lightweight governance** -- Replace heavy gate reviews with continuous visibility (visual management at portfolio level) and exception-based escalation.
- **Rolling planning** -- Replace annual planning cycles with quarterly or continuous planning cadences, enabling faster response to change.

### Portfolio Kanban

Apply kanban principles to the portfolio of initiatives: visualize all work in flight, limit WIP at the portfolio level (stop starting, start finishing), manage flow of initiatives through stages (ideation -> validation -> development -> scaling -> sunset). Portfolio WIP limits prevent the enterprise-level overproduction of starting too many initiatives.

## When This Tradition Applies

- **Problem type:** Type 3 (organizational target condition -- "we want to become a lean enterprise") and Type 4 (enterprise-level innovation strategy).
- **Context signals:** Multi-team coordination, portfolio management, enterprise agile transformation, scaling lean beyond a single team or value stream, governance and compliance, large-scale organizational design.

## Common Mistakes

- **Scaling tools instead of principles.** Installing SAFe, LeSS, or another framework without understanding the underlying lean principles produces ceremony without capability.
- **Applying Horizon 1 governance to Horizon 3 work.** Requiring detailed business cases and ROI projections for exploratory experiments kills innovation. Different horizons need different governance models.
- **Centralizing lean transformation.** A lean enterprise is not built by a transformation office -- it's built by developing lean capability at every level. The transformation office can coordinate, but the capability lives in the teams.
