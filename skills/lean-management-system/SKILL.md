# Lean Management

## Scope

This skill covers lean applied to organizational management -- strategy deployment, alignment, and the management systems that sustain lean operations. Where TPS foundations address the production system and tools address specific methods, lean management addresses how leaders create the conditions for lean to work at scale.

## Core Concepts

### Hoshin Kanri (Strategy Deployment)

A method for aligning an entire organization around a small number of breakthrough objectives while maintaining daily management of ongoing operations. Hoshin kanri (literally "compass management") translates long-term vision into annual breakthrough objectives, then cascades those objectives through the organization with measurable targets at each level.

**Key elements:**
- **True North** -- The organization's long-term vision and purpose. Unchanging directional reference.
- **Breakthrough objectives** -- 2-3 strategic goals for the year that require cross-functional coordination and represent step-change improvement, not incremental gains.
- **Annual hoshin** -- The specific targets and initiatives for each breakthrough objective, with owners and metrics.
- **Catchball** -- The iterative dialogue process for aligning objectives between levels. Senior leadership proposes breakthrough objectives; middle management proposes how to achieve them; the proposal goes back and forth (like catching and throwing a ball) until alignment is reached. Catchball prevents both top-down mandate (which ignores frontline reality) and bottom-up fragmentation (which lacks strategic coherence).
- **Monthly review** -- Regular check on hoshin progress, using A3s to track countermeasures for gaps.

**The hoshin/daily management split:** Hoshin kanri handles breakthrough improvement (the extraordinary). Daily management handles maintaining current standards (the ordinary). Both are essential -- breakthrough without daily management is unsustainable; daily management without breakthrough is stagnation.

### Daily Management

The management practices that sustain current performance and surface problems at the pace of work. Covered in detail by the individual skills: `leader-standard-work`, `daily-accountability`, `visual-management`, `gemba`. Daily management is the foundation that hoshin kanri stands on -- an organization that cannot manage daily operations will not execute breakthrough strategies.

### Catchball as Organizational Learning

Catchball is more than an alignment technique -- it is how the organization learns. When a senior leader's objective meets the frontline's reality through catchball, both perspectives are refined. The leader learns what's feasible; the frontline learns what's strategically important. This bidirectional learning is what distinguishes hoshin kanri from conventional cascading goals (which flow one-way, top-down).

## The Lean Management System

A lean management system integrates the tools into a coherent operating rhythm:

1. **Visual management** makes work status and performance visible.
2. **Daily accountability** reviews performance against standards and targets.
3. **Leader standard work** structures management behavior to sustain the system.
4. **Disciplined problem-solving** (A3, PDCA) addresses problems surfaced by the system.
5. **Hoshin kanri** aligns the whole to strategic direction.

Mann (2014) argues that without a lean management system, lean tools will degrade. The management system IS the sustainability mechanism.
