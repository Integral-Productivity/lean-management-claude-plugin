# Gemba (Go and See)

## What It Is

Gemba means "the actual place" -- the location where value-creating work happens. Gemba practice is the discipline of going to where the work is done, observing the process directly, and building understanding through firsthand experience rather than reports, dashboards, or secondhand accounts.

Gemba is not "management by walking around" (MBWA). MBWA is unstructured; gemba practice has specific intent, defined observation protocols, and follow-through expectations. The purpose is threefold: understand current conditions, build relationships with the people doing the work, and model the organization's commitment to frontline reality.

## When to Use

- **Problem type alignment:** All types. Gemba is foundational to every phase of problem-solving -- you cannot understand a problem you haven't observed directly.
- **Phase alignment:** Especially Phase 2 (Current Condition Grasp), but relevant throughout. Phase 5 (Implementation) also requires gemba presence to observe whether countermeasures are working.

## How to Practice

### Structured Gemba Walk

1. **Define intent.** What are you going to observe? Gemba with no focus produces no insight. Examples: "I want to understand how the intake process actually works," "I want to see where waiting time accumulates," "I want to check whether the new standard is being followed."
2. **Go to where the work happens.** Not a conference room. The actual location -- the floor, the desk, the screen, the service counter, the Slack channel, the code review queue.
3. **Observe before speaking.** Watch the process. Notice the flow, the interruptions, the workarounds. Stand in the "Ohno circle" -- Ohno would draw a circle on the factory floor and have managers stand in it for hours, observing one process until they truly understood it.
4. **Ask questions with curiosity, not judgment.** Core gemba questions:
   - "What are you working on?"
   - "What gets in your way?"
   - "What would make this easier?"
   - "Is there anything you need that you don't have?"
   - "How do you know what to work on next?"
5. **Listen more than you speak.** You are a guest in someone else's workspace. The people doing the work know more about it than you do.
6. **Take notes.** Observations, not opinions. Facts, not interpretations.
7. **Follow up.** If someone tells you about an obstacle, address it. If you observe a problem, act on it. Gemba walks that produce no follow-up lose credibility rapidly -- people stop sharing honestly because nothing changes.

### Cadence

- **Tier 1 leaders (frontline supervisors):** Continuous presence. 60-80% of time at gemba.
- **Tier 2 (middle management):** Daily brief walk + weekly deep observation in one area.
- **Tier 3 (senior leadership):** Monthly structured visit. Quarterly extended gemba with frontline workers.

### Virtual Gemba

For remote/distributed teams, gemba means:
- Observing the actual digital workspace: screen shares, tool walkthroughs, watching someone do their work in real-time
- Sitting in on team standups and retrospectives as a silent observer
- Reviewing actual work artifacts (tickets, PRs, support threads) rather than summary reports
- Scheduled one-on-ones focused on "show me how you do X" rather than status updates

## Common Mistakes

- **Drive-by gemba.** Walking through quickly, waving, leaving. This is performative presence, not observation.
- **Gemba as inspection.** Going to the floor to catch people doing things wrong. This kills psychological safety and ensures you'll never see the real problems.
- **Solving problems during the walk.** Gemba is for observing and understanding. Problem-solving comes later, using the structured framework. On-the-spot fixes are usually symptoms-only solutions.
- **Sporadic visits.** Occasional gemba signals that leadership only shows up when something is wrong. Regular cadence normalizes presence.
- **Only talking to managers.** Gemba means talking to the people who do the work -- operators, developers, support agents, technicians -- not their managers.

## Relationship to Other Tools

- **leader-standard-work:** Gemba walks are a core LSW activity at every tier.
- **job-relations:** Gemba is where relationships are built -- regular presence, genuine questions, follow-through.
- **visual-management:** Gemba includes reviewing visual management boards -- are they current? Are they being used?
- **standardized-work:** Process confirmation (observing work against the standard) happens at gemba.
- **lean-problem-solving:** Gemba is the primary method for grasping the current condition (Phase 2).
